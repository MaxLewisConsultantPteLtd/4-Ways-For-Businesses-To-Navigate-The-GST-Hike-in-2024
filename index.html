4 Ways For Businesses To Navigate The GST Hike in 2024

The impending Goods and Services Tax (GST) hike in Singapore in 2024 presents a significant challenge for businesses, particularly small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs). As the tax rate is set to increase from 8% to 9%, businesses need to strategically navigate through this financial terrain to sustain and grow.

Read on as we explore four essential ways for businesses to effectively navigate the GST hike in 2024.

1. Voluntary GST registration

A crucial approach for businesses, especially new enterprises with taxable supplies under S$1 million annually, is to consider voluntary GST registration. By voluntarily registering for GST, these businesses can reclaim the GST incurred on their expenses, effectively counteracting the cost increases resulting from the GST hike. This strategy becomes a powerful tool to preserve the bottom line, ensuring that the impact of the tax increase is mitigated.

However, the decision to opt for voluntary GST registration requires careful consideration. Businesses must assess how this strategy may influence their pricing and customer perception. While the move can significantly lower costs for the businesses themselves, it may translate to higher prices for consumers who are not GST-registered. Businesses need to evaluate the price sensitivity of their goods or services to determine if customers will be deterred by potential price increases.

2. Automating tax processes

With the GST hike in 2024, the importance of automating tax processes becomes more critical than ever. GST-registered businesses must implement proper tax procedures to avoid costly non-compliance penalties. Automation not only ensures compliance but also enhances overall efficiency in dealing with complex tax calculations and filings.

The Productivity Solutions Grant offers businesses the opportunity to procure automation technologies at a subsidised rate, making it more accessible for SMEs. By automating tax processes, businesses can redirect their focus towards more productive tasks, such as revenue generation, instead of spending time on labour-intensive accounting tasks like tax filing. This not only helps in navigating the challenges posed by the GST hike but also boosts overall business productivity.

3. Leveraging GST relief schemes

GST relief schemes like the Major Exporter Scheme (MES) is a strategic response to the GST hike in 2024. MES emerges as a strategic lifeline for businesses navigating the impending GST hike in 2024, especially those deeply involved in import and export activities. Administered by the Inland Revenue Authority of Singapore, the MES facilitates businesses by suspending upfront GST payments on non-dutiable imported goods. This pivotal feature allows businesses to defer GST payments until the goods are removed for local use or consumption, effectively easing cash flow pressures. By providing a practical and beneficial solution, the MES empowers businesses to optimise their financial strategies amidst the challenges presented by the GST hike.

4. Diversification and cost optimisation

In addition to specific strategies to directly address the GST hike, businesses should consider diversifying their revenue streams and optimising costs. Exploring new market segments, offering additional services or products, and finding cost-effective operational methods can help cushion the impact of the GST increase.

This proactive approach not only prepares businesses for the GST hike but also strengthens their overall market position. By diversifying, businesses become less reliant on a single revenue source, making them more resilient to external economic changes, such as tax rate hikes. Additionally, optimising costs ensures that businesses are operating efficiently, allowing them to navigate challenges while maintaining profitability.
